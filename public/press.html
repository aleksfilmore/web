Story Angles

The Indie-Author Angle

Aleks Filmore's The Worst Boyfriends Ever started as breakup therapy and became an indie success, showing how self-publishing is giving queer voices new autonomy.

"I didn't want revenge. I wanted closure that didn't require anyone else to give it to me."

Heartbreak Canon Angle

From chaos (The Worst Boyfriends Ever) to silence (Aftertaste) to accountability (Am I The Red Flag?), Filmore is turning emotional fallout into a literary arc.

"It started as a breakup book. It became a pattern-recognition exercise."

Emotional Realism Angle

His writing skips self-help tropes and instead treats pain with irony and precision, more autopsy than affirmation.

"I wanted love to fix me, not find me. That's how you end up in stories like this one."

Red Flag Angle

His upcoming book Am I the Red Flag? turns the gaze inward, exploring accountability with humor, not shame.

"Every relationship is a mirror. Sometimes the reflection isn't flattering, but it's still you."
